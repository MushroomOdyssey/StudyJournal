The <img> element works similarly to <a>, but instead of bringing you to the content, it brings the content to you!

The structure is recognizable - tag, attribute, value: <img src=" ">

But we don't use a closing tag with <img> - it's self-closing. There is no such thing as </img>


Let's take a closer look at this element:

<img> defines a placeholder for an image to be embedded into the document.

src=" " is an attribute of <img> that takes a value.

And just like in the anchor element, the value entered between the qoutation marks " " is the destination URL of our desired image!


Now, FINALLY, let's combine absolutely everything that we talked about in Day 3, along with a little bonus knowledge:


Behold:

<a href="../pages/kittens.html"><img src="../images/OIG1.jpeg" alt="A kitten in a wizard's cloak and hat casting a spell" height="500" width="500"></a>

Let's simplify that really quick.

  • <a href="">
    The anchor start tag
    • <img src="">
      The image element with some bonus alt text and display constraints
      Note that this image element is nested within the anchor element
  • </a>
    The anchor close tag

By nesting the <img> element within the <a> </a> element, the <img> then becomes a child of <a>, which means that, in our example, the <img> becomes the content of the anchor.
This turns the whole image into a link!
